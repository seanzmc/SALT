# Objective

Review the provided business-preparation context and produce a refined list of actionable tasks to prepare for opening a medspa business in Lakeland, Florida.

# Core Instructions

- Convert the provided information into a task list that is practical, specific, and action-oriented.
- Focus on tasks that are meaningful for execution, avoiding both overly generic items and overly granular microtasks.
- Aim for a balanced level of detail: each task should be clear, independently actionable, and have obvious success criteria.
- Use the business context, partner roles, and clinic specialty to make the task list relevant.
- If required context is missing, do not invent specifics; write the most defensible actionable task supported by the provided context.
- Remove duplicate tasks, including tasks that substantially overlap in meaning even if they are worded differently.

# Business Context

- The business is a medspa located in Lakeland, FL.
- The location has already been leased.
- The build-out planning process will take place in the next few weeks to determine layout.
- The clinic specialty is laser treatment for both skin services and hair removal.

# Partner Roles

- There are three primary managing partners completing the tasks:
  - **Steve**: primarily a silent investor, but brings business connections and experience. He will handle a majority of the initial business operations, including finalizing the lease and providing working capital.
  - **Caitlin**: involved in day-to-day operations, certified in laser treatment, has a business degree, and has more management experience. She will be responsible for more of the back-of-house business functions.
  - **Jesse**: involved in day-to-day operations, certified in laser treatment, and more experienced with customer relations, networking, and front-of-house responsibilities.

# Task Quality Standard

- The final output should be a list of actionable tasks devoid of generic items that would only lead to a list of sub-items.
- Prefer tasks that are scoped tightly enough to act on immediately, but broad enough to avoid unnecessary fragmentation.
- Do not include placeholder-style or vague tasks.
- Exclude tasks that are duplicative or materially redundant with other tasks in the list.

# Example Guidance

- Bad task: `Confirm recommended room program for the 1,600 sq ft clinic.`
  - Reason: references something that may not exist and is too generic to be truly actionable.
- Good task: `Draft lean-opening layout with future room capacity.`
  - Reason: single task with obvious success criteria.

# Output Requirements

- Produce a clean list of actionable tasks only.
- Ensure the tasks reflect the actual stage of the business: leased location, upcoming build-out planning, laser-focused specialty, and defined partner responsibilities.
- Keep the wording concise and practical.
- Return only the task list, with one task per line and no headings, explanations, or extra commentary.
- Before finalizing, verify that every task is actionable, non-duplicative, and grounded in the provided context.
